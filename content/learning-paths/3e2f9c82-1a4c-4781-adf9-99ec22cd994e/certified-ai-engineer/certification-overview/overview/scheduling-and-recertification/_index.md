This lesson covers the logistics around the DO-CAIE: scheduling, attempts, results, and keeping
your certification current as DigitalOcean's AI platform evolves.

## Scheduling your exam

1. Complete the recommended learning paths and the practice questions in this track.
2. From the certification page, choose **Start the DO-CAIE exam** to launch the proctored written
   exam, or **Submit capstone** to open the capstone workflow.
3. You can take the written exam and the capstone in either order, but both must be passed within a
   **12-month window** of your first attempt.

## Attempts and retakes

- If you do not pass the written exam, you may retake it after a **14-day** waiting period.
- A second retake requires a further **30-day** wait. There is no annual cap beyond these cool-downs.
- A failed capstone can be revised and resubmitted once per review cycle, using reviewer feedback.

## Results

- Written-exam results are available immediately, with a per-domain score breakdown so you know
  where to improve.
- Capstone results are returned within **5 business days** with written feedback.
- On passing both, you receive a **digital badge** and a verifiable certificate.

## Validity and recertification

The DO-CAIE is valid for **two years**. DigitalOcean's AI services change quickly — new models,
the Inference Router, evolving knowledge-base connectors, and Meshery features — so recertification
keeps the credential meaningful.

To recertify, you can either:

- Pass a shorter **delta exam** that covers only what has changed since your certification, or
- Complete a published **recertification capstone** reflecting the current platform.

## Staying current between exams

- Follow the DigitalOcean [Gradient AI Platform docs](https://docs.digitalocean.com/products/gradient-ai-platform/)
  and product changelogs.
- Track [Meshery](https://docs.meshery.io/) releases for new integrations and Design capabilities.
- Re-run your capstone periodically against the latest models and measure the difference with
  Evaluations and Meshery Performance Profiles.

You are now ready to dive into the domain-by-domain **Exam Domains Study Guide**.
